# Rules of Golf — Unplayable reference (starter)

This is the reference the AI model reads to make its ruling. It deliberately covers only
the situations golfers actually hit out on the course. Expand it as you find gaps during
Phase 0 grading.

Rules referenced here are the current (2023) Rules of Golf as published by the R&A and the USGA.

## Rule 9 — Ball played as it lies
The ball is normally played as it lies. You do not get to improve a lie unless a specific
rule gives you relief. If your ball is in a tricky but legal spot and no relief rule applies,
the ruling is play it as it lies.
- ruling_type: play_as_it_lies
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-9

## Rule 12 — Bunkers
You may now remove loose impediments and movable obstructions in a bunker. You must not
test the sand or ground your club in the sand right behind or in front of the ball. Embedded
ball relief does NOT apply in a bunker. Unplayable relief inside a bunker has an extra option
(back-on-the-line within the bunker for one penalty, or outside the bunker for two penalties).
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-12

## Rule 13 — Putting greens
On the green you may mark, lift and clean your ball, repair damage including spike marks and
old hole plugs, and there is no penalty if your ball or marker is accidentally moved.
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-13

## Rule 14 — Procedure for relief (how to drop)
When taking relief you drop from knee height, the ball must land in and come to rest in the
relief area, and you re-drop if it does not. This is the "how", not the "whether".
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-14

## Rule 15 — Loose impediments and movable obstructions
- 15.1 Loose impediments (leaves, stones, twigs, dead animals): you may remove them anywhere,
  including in a bunker. If removing one causes your ball to move, there may be a penalty in
  some locations. Free of charge to remove.
- 15.2 Movable obstructions (rakes, bottles, signage, movable cart-path debris): free relief —
  move the obstruction, or if the ball moves, replace it.
- ruling_type: free_relief
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-15

## Rule 16 — Abnormal course conditions, dangerous animals, embedded ball
- 16.1 Free relief from abnormal course conditions in the general area: immovable obstructions
  (cart paths, sprinkler heads, fixed signs, fences that are obstructions), ground under repair
  (GUR), temporary (casual) water, and animal holes. You get the nearest point of complete
  relief, no penalty. Not available when the ball is in a penalty area.
- 16.2 Dangerous animal condition (e.g. a snake, a bees' nest): free relief.
- 16.3 Embedded ball: free relief if your ball is embedded in its own pitch-mark in the GENERAL
  AREA. NOT available in a bunker, in a penalty area, or when embedded in sand outside the
  general area cut to fairway height or less.
- ruling_type: free_relief
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-16

## Rule 17 — Penalty areas (red and yellow)
A penalty area is marked by stakes or lines, red or yellow.
- Yellow penalty area: two options for one penalty stroke — stroke-and-distance, or back-on-the-line.
- Red penalty area: the same two options plus lateral relief within two club-lengths of where the
  ball last crossed the edge, no nearer the hole, for one penalty stroke.
- If you can and want to play it as it lies from within the penalty area, you may, with no penalty.
- ruling_type: penalty (unless played as it lies)
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-17

## Rule 18 — Stroke and distance; ball lost or out of bounds
- 18.1 You may always take stroke-and-distance relief for one penalty stroke.
- 18.2 If your ball is out of bounds (beyond the white stakes/line) or cannot be found within
  three minutes, it is lost: stroke-and-distance, one penalty stroke. (Many clubs use the local
  rule allowing an alternative two-penalty drop near where the ball was lost or went out of bounds —
  note this only if the club has adopted it.)
- ruling_type: penalty
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-18

## Rule 19 — Unplayable ball
You alone decide your ball is unplayable, anywhere except a penalty area. For one penalty stroke
you have three options: stroke-and-distance, back-on-the-line, or lateral relief within two
club-lengths no nearer the hole. In a bunker there is an extra back-on-the-line-outside-the-bunker
option for two penalty strokes.
- ruling_type: penalty
- URL: https://www.randa.org/rog/the-rules-of-golf/rule-19
